First off, thank you for your interest in donating to Texas Radiation Online. You may either use your credit card or send a wire transfer using PayPal. There has been a PO Box provided for people to send in material through snail mail, but due to a lack of a budget, we can no longer provide this option.

Before proceeding, it is really important to emphasize that Texas Radiation Online does not have a 501(c)(3) status. This means that donations which are provided are not currently tax deductable.


Sometime in the future, we will obtain 501(c)(3) status. We feel that the decision to operate under another organization's non-profit filing status is one which must not be taken lightly, and might imply more of an affiliation symbolically or in project responsibility than exists in reality. Under careful consideration, a decision in regards to providing 501(c)(3) will be made in the future.

• Keeping the project going.
This is a project with a shoestring budget and without an office. Occassional long-distance calls must be made around Texas to make it function. The project also needs a PO Box for people who are scared of donating with online transactions. [I won't give out my family's address for this- too risky]. Much of the books and office equipment which support the project have to be kept in a storage room, which has a monthly fee.

• Covering the basics.
Unemployment is bad in Austin Texas. There is little or no income for the author to maintain the project on. Occassionally, a bit of contract work employs the author, but is very rare and not enough to get back into an apartment. Currently, he is staying on his family's couch, so any money helps to cover basic necessities. [this is a mixed blessing- this project would have never developed if unemployment wasn't so bad the last two years!]
